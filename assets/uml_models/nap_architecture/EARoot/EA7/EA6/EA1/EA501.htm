<b>Title</b>: Regulation (EU) 2023/1804 on the deployment of alternative fuels infrastructure<br/><b>Reference</b>: AFIR<br/><b>Type</b>: Legal Specification<br/><b>Full Name and Hyperlink</b>: <a href="https://transport.ec.europa.eu/transport-themes/clean-transport/alternative-fuels-sustainable-mobility-europe/alternative-fuels-infrastructure_en"><font color="#0000ff"><u>Regulation (EU) 2023/1804</u></font></a><br/><b>Contextual Use</b>: The Regulation (EU) 2023/1804 on the deployment of alternative fuels infrastructure sets mandatory national targets for EU Member States to deploy publicly accessible alternative fuels infrastructure, particularly for electricity and hydrogen, for road vehicles, vessels moored at the quayside, and stationary aircraft. It includes common rules for user information, data provision, and payment requirements, and mandates the Commission to adopt delegated acts to ensure interoperability of infrastructure by mandating technical specifications based on European standards. This regulation supports the transition towards more sustainable modes of transport and aims to decarbonize the transport sector across the EU.<br/><b>Document Number</b>: 2023/1804<br/><b>Publication Date</b>: 13 September 2023<br/><b>Related Documents</b>:<br/><ul>
<li><b>Directive 2014/94/EU</b>: Previous directive on the deployment of alternative fuels infrastructure, repealed by Regulation (EU) 2023/1804.</li><li><b>Regulation (EU) 2018/2001</b>: Sets a market share target for renewables in transport fuels.</li><li><b>Regulation (EU) 2019/631</b>: CO2 emission performance standards for new passenger cars and light-duty vehicles.</li><li><b>Regulation (EU) 2019/1242</b>: CO2 emission performance standards for certain new heavy-duty vehicles.</li></ul><p/>
