<b>Technical specifications are used to specify: </b><br/><b>Performance</b>: Outline the required performance metrics, such as speed, capacity, and reliability. They strictly define the measurable criteria that the system must meet, without specifying the functions or design details.<br/><b>Technical</b>: Provide detailed technical information, including materials, dimensions, and manufacturing processes. They focus exclusively on the technical aspects and physical characteristics of the system or component.<br/><b>Design</b>: Detail the design aspects, including drawings, schematics, and design principles. They concentrate on the visual and structural design elements, without specifying the technical details or performance metrics.<br/><b>Interface</b>: Define how different systems or components will interact with each other, including communication protocols and data formats. They focus on the interaction and integration points, without detailing the functions, performance, or design aspects.<br/><br/>
