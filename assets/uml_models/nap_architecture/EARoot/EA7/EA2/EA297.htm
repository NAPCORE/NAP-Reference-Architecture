<b>Subject matter and scope</b><br/>This Regulation establishes the specifications necessary to ensure compatibility, interoperability and continuity for the deployment and operational use of data and procedures for the provision, where possible, of road safety-related minimum universal traffic information free of charge to users on a Union level in accordance with Directive 2010/40/EU.<br/>It shall apply to the provision of road safety-related minimum universal traffic information services on the trans-European road network.<br/><br/><b>List of road safety-related events or conditions</b><br/>The events or conditions covered by the road safety-related minimum universal traffic information service shall consist of at least one of the following categories:<br/>(a) temporary slippery road;<br/>(b) animal, people, obstacles, debris on the road;<br/>(c) unprotected accident area;<br/>(d) short-term road works;<br/>(e) reduced visibility;<br/>(f) wrong-way driver;<br/>(g) unmanaged blockage of a road;<br/>(h) exceptional weather conditions.<br/><br/><b>Information content</b><br/>1. The information provided on the road safety-related events or conditions shall include the following items:<br/>(a) location of the event or the condition;<br/>(b) the category of event or condition as referred to in Article 3 and, where appropriate, short description of it;<br/>(c) driving behaviour advice, where appropriate.<br/>2. The information shall be withdrawn if the event or condition cease to subsist, or shall be modified if there is a change in the event or condition.<br/><br/><b>Detection of events or conditions and collection of data</b><br/>For the sole purposes of providing the information service, public and private road operators and/or service providers shall set up or use the means to detect events or identify conditions, and shall collect the relevant road safety-related traffic data.<br/>The deployment of these means shall comply with the conditions and requirements set out in national law.<br/><br/><b>Availability, exchange and reuse of data</b><br/>1. Public and/or private road operators and/or service providers shall share and exchange the data they collect pursuant to Article 6. For that purpose, they shall make these data available in the DATEX II (CEN/TS 16157) format or any fully compatible and interoperable with DATEX II machine-readable format through an access point.<br/>2. Member States shall manage a national access point to the data referred to in paragraph 1, which regroups the access points established by public and/or private road operators and/or service providers operating on their territory.<br/>3. These data shall be accessible for exchange and reuse by any user of road safety-related minimum universal traffic information:<br/>(a) on a non-discriminatory basis;<br/>(b) within the Union irrespective of the Member State of establishment;<br/>(c) in accordance with access rights and procedures defined in Directive 2003/98/EC;<br/>(d) within a timeframe that ensures the timely provision of the information service;<br/>(e) through the national access point.<br/>4. Public and private road operators and service providers shall ensure the timely renewal and quality of data made available through their access point.<br/><br/><b>Dissemination of information</b><br/>1. Public road operators, service providers and broadcasters dedicated to traffic information shall provide road safety-related minimum universal traffic information to end users prior to the provision of any other non-safety-related traffic information.<br/>2. The information service shall fulfil the following conditions:<br/>(a) it shall be provided in such a way as to ensure the widest reach of end users concerned by the given event or condition referred to in Article 3;<br/>(b) it shall be made available by public and/or private road operators and/or service providers and/or broadcasters dedicated to traffic information, where possible free of charge to end users.<br/>3. Public and private road operators and service providers shall collaborate to harmonise the presentation of the content of the information provided to end users.<br/>They shall inform end users of the existence of the information service and its coverage.<br/><br/>https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=CELEX:32013R0886<br/><br/>
