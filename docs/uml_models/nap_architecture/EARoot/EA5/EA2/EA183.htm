<b>Parties</b>: member state (MS) and European Commission (EC)<br/><b>Objective</b>: To set up obligations of the MS towards the EC and to the national stakeholders.<br/><b>Form</b>: the ITS Directive and Delegated Regulations <br/><b>Obligations</b>:<br/><ul>
<li>EC to MS: ---</li><li>MS to EC:</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>Implementation of the law into a local law (for the Directives) including an enforcement process</li><li>Periodical reporting of the progress =#gt; to fully comply with the requirements (data types?) toward</li><li>Establishing processes for not compliance within the MS with clear responsibilities</li><li>Establishing NAP (name in the local law responsible party for establishing it or name it directly)</li><li>Establishing NB (name in the local law responsible party for establishing it or name it directly, create a framework for its operation = the compliance assessment process)</li></ul>
<br/>The obligations of the MS are related to the following European laws:<br/><br/><ul>
<li>2010/40/EU (ITS Directive)</li><li>886/2013 (SRTI) </li><li>885/2013 (SSTP)</li><li>2015/962 (old RTTI)</li><li>2017/1926 (MMTIS)</li><li>2022/670 (RTTI update)</li><li>2023/2661 (ITS Directive update)</li><li>2024/490 (MMTIS update)</li></ul>
<br/>Individual obligations are as follows:<br/><br/><ul>
<li>Each Member State <u>shall set up a national access point</u>. [2015/962 article 3, 2022/670 article 3, 2017/1926 article 3]</li><li>National access points that have been set up to comply with the requirements arising from other delegated acts adopted under Directive 2010/40/EU may be used, if deemed appropriate by the Member States, as national access points. [2015/962 article 3]</li><li>Two or more Member States may set up a common access point. [2015/962 article 3]</li></ul><p/>
