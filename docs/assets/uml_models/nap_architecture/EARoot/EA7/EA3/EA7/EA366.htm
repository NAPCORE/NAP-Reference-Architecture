NAPs should clearly describe the terms and conditions for data re-use and their operational procedures to avoid disputes and facilitate impact estimation. This includes providing prespecified licenses for data providers and clear instructions on user roles, registration, and dataset updates. NAPs should also endorse high-quality data that complies with ITS Directive requirements, allowing data providers to indicate data quality. Promoting self-declarations from data providers can aid compliance activities. Additionally, NAPs should cover all data types in the ITS Directive, serving as a single access point for Intelligent Transport System services.<br/>KPIs:<br/><ul>
<li>Clarity of terms and conditions for data re-use</li><li>Clear operational procedures</li><li>Provision of prespecified licenses</li><li>Endorsement of high-quality data</li><li>Promotion of self-declarations from data providers</li><li>Coverage of all data types in the ITS Directive</li></ul>
