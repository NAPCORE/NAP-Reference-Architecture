<b>Subject matter and scope </b><br/>This Regulation establishes the specifications necessary to ensure compatibility, interoperability and continuity for the deployment and operational use of information services for safe and secure parking places for trucks and commercial vehicles on a Union level in accordance with Directive 2010/40/EU.<br/>It shall apply to the provision of information services situated on the trans-European road network (TERN).<br/><br/><b>Requirements for the provision of information services</b><br/>1. Member States shall designate areas where traffic and security conditions require the deployment of information services on the safe and secure parking places. <br/>They shall also define priority zones where dynamic information will be provided.<br/>2. The provision of information services shall fulfil the requirements set out in Articles 4 to 7.<br/><br/><b>Data collection</b><br/>Data on safe and secure public and private parking areas describing the parking facility, to be provided to the users, shall be collected and supplied by public or private parking operators and service providers. The data to be collected shall be easy to provide, including remotely, by any relevant means, in order to facilitate a distant collection by all parking operators. Public or private parking operators and service providers shall use DATEX II ( 1 ) profiles or other internationally compatible formats in order to ensure interoperability of the information services across the Union.<br/>The data to be collected shall be the following:<br/>1. Static data related to the parking areas, including (where<br/>applicable)<br/>— Identification information of parking area (name and address of the truck parking area (limited to 200 characters))<br/>— Location information of the entry point in the parking area (latitude/longitude) (20 + 20 characters)<br/>— Primary road identifier1/direction (20 characters/20 characters), and Primary road identifier2/direction (20 characters/20 characters) if same parking accessible from two different roads<br/>— If needed, the indication of the Exit to be taken (limited to 100 characters)/Distance from primary road (integer 3) km or miles<br/>— Total number of free parking places for trucks (integer 3)<br/>— Price and currency of parking places (300 characters)<br/>2. Information on safety and equipment of the parking area<br/>— Description of security, safety and service equipment of the parking including national classification if one is applied (500 characters)<br/>— Number of parking places for refrigerated goods vehicles (numerical 4 digits)<br/>— Information on specific equipment or services for specific goods vehicles and other (300 characters)<br/>Contact information of the parking operator:<br/>— Name and surname (up to 100 characters)<br/>— Telephone number (up to 20 characters)<br/>— E-mail address (up to 50 characters)<br/>— Consent of the operator to make his contact information public (Yes/No)<br/>3. Dynamic data on availability of parking places including whether a parking is: full, closed or number of free places which are available.<br/><br/><b>Sharing and exchange of data</b><br/>1. Public or private parking operators and service providers shall share and exchange data referred to in paragraph 1 of Article 4. For these purposes they shall use DATEX II (CEN/TS 16157) format or any DATEX II compatible international machine-readable format. Data shall be accessible for exchange and reuse by any public or private information service provider and/or parking operator on a non-discriminatory basis, and in accordance with access rights and procedures defined in Directive 2003/98/EC.<br/>2. The static data shall be accessible through a national or international access point.<br/>3. For dynamic data, Member States (or national authorities) shall be responsible for setting up and managing a central national or international point of access referencing all individual single points of access of each truck parking operator and/or service provider on their territory in the interests of users.<br/>4. Member States may contribute to an international access point by providing data and ensuring that its quality is in conformity with the requirements in Article 7.<br/>5. Charges for access to, exchange of, and reuse of public or private dynamic data shall remain reasonable as referred to in the PSI Directive.<br/>6. Public and private parking operators and/or service providers shall periodically send their static collected data to the national or international access point through appropriate electronic means no less than once a year for static data referred to in Article 4(1).<br/>For dynamic data public and private operators and/or services providers shall update their information referred to in Article 4(3) no less than once every 15 minutes.<br/><br/><b>Dissemination of information</b><br/>Service providers collecting information at a specific location shall display:<br/>— at least the next two safe and secure parking places along a corridor within approximately 100 kilometres,<br/>— the availability of parking places in a priority zone in at least the next two parking areas within approximately 100 kilometres.<br/>The dissemination of information shall be consistent with the Vienna convention where a Member State has signed it. In-vehicle application should have a robust human machine interface in order to avoid driver distraction and fatigue. Parking operators and/or service providers shall inform the users about the launch of any new information service for safe and secure parking by any communication means they find appropriate.<br/><br/><br/><a href="https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=celex%3A32013R0885"><font color="#0000ff"><u>https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=celex%3A32013R0885</u></font></a><br/><br/>
