<b>Parties</b>: Member State (MS) and selected Content Consumer (CC); Only digital map producers, <font color="#333333">service providers, and </font>broadcasters are relevant Content Consumers for this "agreement". <br/><b>Objective</b>: To identify CCs and set up obligations of the CCs.<br/><b>Form</b>: National law implementing the ITS Directive and direct execution of EU Regulations and Delegated Acts.<br/><b>Obligations:</b><br/>#gt; MS to CC: ---<br/>#gt; CC to MS (EC):<br/><ul>
<li>To report to the content provider (CP) any inaccuracies related to the data without delay</li><li>To include any traffic circulation plans and traffic regulations and restrictions, temporary traffic management measures developed by the competent authorities, and made accessible via NAP, in the service. [Service providers]</li><li>When presenting information to end users, processes relevant data updates on infrastructure, regulations, and restrictions [digital map producers], data updates on the state of the network / the real-time use of the network within a timeframe fitting to the reliable and effective use of the data in real-time traffic information services. [service providers]</li><li>To provide SRTI information service to ensure the widest reach of end users concerned, where possible free of charge to end users.</li></ul>
<br/><b>Reference to the EU law:</b><br/>The obligations of the CC are related to the following laws:<br/><ul>
<li>National law transposing 2010/40/EU (ITS Directive)</li><li>886/2013 (SRTI)</li><li>2015/962 (RTTI valid until 31. December 2024)</li><li>2022/670 (RTTI  in force from 1. January 2025)</li><li>National law transposing 2023/2661 (ITS Directive update)</li></ul>
<br/><b>Selected references to the law:</b><br/><ul>
<li>Data users using the data referred to in paragraph 1 and data holders <b>shall</b> collaborate in order to ensure that any inaccuracies related to the data are signalled without delay to the data holder from which the data originates. [2022/670, Art 4, par 3; Art 5, par 3; Art 6, par 3]</li><li>Service providers <b>shall</b> process and include, without additional costs to the end-user, in the relevant services they provide, any traffic circulation plans and traffic regulations and restrictions / temporary traffic management measures developed by the competent authorities and made accessible via the national or common access point in a digital machine-readable format. [2022/670, Art 5, par 4, Art 6, par 4]</li><li>When digital map producers and service providers present information to end users, they <b>shall</b> ensure that relevant data updates on infrastructure / on regulations and restrictions are processed within a timeframe fitting to the reliable and effective use of the data in real-time traffic information services. [2022/670, Art 8, par 4, Art 9, par 4]</li><li>When service providers present information to end users, they <b>shall</b> ensure that relevant data updates on the state of the network / the real-time use of the network are processed within a timeframe fitting to the reliable and effective use of the data in real-time traffic information services. [2022/670, Art 10, par 5, Art 11, par 3]</li><li>The accessibility and regular update of data by road authorities and road operators are essential for enabling the production of up-to-date and accurate digital maps that are a key asset for reliable ITS applications. The digital map producers <b>should</b> be encouraged to integrate relevant data updates into their existing map and map update services in a timely manner. In order to comply with public policies such as road safety, service providers and digital map producers <b>should</b> collaborate with public authorities to correct inaccuracies in their data. [2022/670, preamble, 15]<ul>
<li>The information service shall fulfil the following conditions: [2013/886 Art. 8, par 2]<br/>(a) it <b>shall</b> be provided in such a way as to ensure the widest reach of end users concerned by the given event or condition referred to in Article 3;<br/>(b) it <b>shall</b> be made available by public and/or private road operators and/or service providers and/or broadcasters dedicated to traffic information, where possible free of charge to end users.</li></ul>
</li><br/></ul><p/>
