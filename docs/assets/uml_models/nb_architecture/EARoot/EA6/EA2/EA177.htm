Member State authorities shall appoint a NB/CA and make sure that this NB/CA is properly authorised, <br/>according to national regulations, to receive and process self-declarations.<br/>The NB/CA will need the following preparation before being ready to receive self-declarations:<br/>· Identify and organise resources for receiving and processing self-declarations and for <br/>performing compliance assessment and random inspections.<br/>· Develop an internal process for receiving and processing self-declarations, taking into account <br/>both European regulation and contextual factors present in the Member State such as the<br/>number of service or data providers expected to submit a self-declaration and national <br/>administrative law.<br/>· Develop a plan on how to identify data and service providers covered by the delegated <br/>regulation and how the NB/CA will approach the stakeholders.<br/>· Provide national templates for self-declarations and accompanying documents.<br/>· Compile Member State specific instructions for data and service providers on how to fill in and <br/>submit a self-declaration and related accompanying documents.<br/>After the NB/CA has been nominated and necessary preparations have been carried out, it will provide <br/>information to potential data and service providers on their obligation to submit a self-declaration and <br/>provide instructions on how to fill in and submit a self-declaration. The NB/CA may inform the <br/>stakeholders with different methods such as email campaigns, sending letters, publishing information <br/>on the NAP website or arranging workshops. <br/>To identify the relevant actors obligated to submit a self-declaration, the NB/CA may use procedures <br/>specific to its Member State. These may include e.g., utilisation of the contacts of the NAP operator. <br/>Good knowledge of the national mobility landscape is required to identify the stakeholders potentially <br/>having an obligation to submit a self-declaration. To increase its knowledge and to build a more <br/>complete picture of the mobility landscape, the NB/CA may collaborate with local or regional transport <br/>authorities. When exchanging information between authorities, GDPR compliance is necessary.<br/>After being contacted by the NB/CA, the data or service provider submits a data set or service to the <br/>National Access Point (NAP), international access point or content access point as required by the <br/>applicable delegated regulation. After submitting the service or data set, the provider fills in and <br/>submits the self-declaration form to the NB/CA. This can take place either electronically (i.e., by email <br/>or by indicating compliance via a checkbox) or in paper form. <br/>The processing of a self-declaration form starts with verification of the contact details of the data or <br/>service provider and checking whether the NB/CA is responsible and acknowledged for the respective <br/>delegated regulation. In case of incomplete or inconsistent contact details, the NB/CA may request the <br/>data or service provider to update its contact details. If the stakeholder does not provide the missing contact details or does not correct erroneous contact details, the NB/CA may stop processing the selfdeclaration. After verification of contact details, the NB/CA verifies the completeness of the selfdeclaration. In practice, this means checking that all mandatory fields of the self-declaration form have <br/>been filled in, and all required accompanying documents have been provided. If needed, the NB/CA<br/>may request the data or service provider to update the self-declaration and complete the missing <br/>parts. After a complete self-declaration has been received, the NB/CA can provide an <br/>acknowledgement to the data or service provider. After receiving and processing the self-declaration, <br/>the NB/CA might update the results to a registry/database (which is not compulsory) of processed selfdeclarations. At the same time, the NB/CA may also update the registry of compliant services and data <br/>sets (optional), if it has been established in the Member State. Contents of these registries will be the <br/>basis for periodic reporting related to the ITS Directive and delegated regulations in the Member State <br/>and at the European level. The random inspections that are carried out periodically at pre-determined <br/>intervals also check the collected self-declarations and descriptions of compliant services and data sets. In the following figures, digital map producers and broadcasters are included in “data or <br/>service provider”<br/>
